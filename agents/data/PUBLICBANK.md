# Public Bank Berhad

## Bank Identity

- bank_id: publicbank
- display_name: Public Bank
- aliases: Public Bank Berhad, PBB, PB
- country: Malaysia
- official_hotline: 1-800-22-5555
- official_hotline_international: +603-2177-3555
- fraud_hotline: 03-2177-3555 (report unauthorized transactions)
- official_website: https://www.pbebank.com

## Things Public Bank Will NEVER Do On a Phone Call

- Ask for your full TAC (Transaction Authorization Code) or OTP
- Ask for your PBe username or password
- Ask for your full credit/debit card number, CVV, or expiry date
- Ask for your full IC number over the phone
- Ask you to transfer money to a "safe account," "holding account," or "Bank Negara account"
- Ask you to install remote access or screen-sharing apps
- Ask you to click on links sent via SMS or WhatsApp to verify your identity
- Threaten you with arrest, court action, or account closure to create urgency
- Ask you to download a non-official app to "secure" your account
- Call you from a personal mobile number or WhatsApp number claiming to be Public Bank staff

## Things Public Bank May Legitimately Do

- Call you to verify a transaction that was flagged by their fraud monitoring system
- Ask you to confirm the last 4 digits of your account number or card number
- Ask basic security questions for identity verification (date of birth, partial IC)
- Request you to visit a branch with your IC for account-related issues
- Send official SMS from "PBeMobile" or "PublicBank" sender IDs
- Ask you to call back the official hotline number for further assistance
- Notify you via the official PB engage app about account alerts
- Send transaction notifications through the PB engage app
- Ask you to verify a SecureSign approval on your PB engage app

## Public Bank-Specific Features the Caller Should Know About

If a caller claims to be from Public Bank but does not know these, they may be fake:

- Public Bank uses **SecureSign** as the primary transaction authorization method via the PB engage app — not SMS TAC for most transactions
- Public Bank's mobile banking app is called **PB engage** — a fake caller might call it by the wrong name or not know it exists
- Public Bank's online banking platform is **PBe** at pbebank.com — not any other domain
- Public Bank offers **eFD (electronic Fixed Deposit)** through PBe — a scammer might not know the correct product names
- Public Bank's **auto-lock feature** disables online banking access after multiple failed login attempts — a real officer would know this
- Public Bank uses **PB SecureOTP** only as a backup when SecureSign is unavailable — if a caller demands OTP as the primary method, that is suspicious

## Standard Call Procedures

- Public Bank's outbound calls will come from a recognizable official number, not a personal mobile number
- Public Bank will never demand immediate action — they will advise you to verify by calling back the official hotline
- If Public Bank detects fraud on your account, they will block the card or account first and then contact you to verify
- Public Bank staff will never ask you to keep the call secret from family or friends
- Public Bank will never instruct you to make a transaction or transfer during an active call
- Public Bank will never conference or transfer your call to "Bank Negara," "PDRM," or any government agency — these institutions do not operate this way
- Public Bank staff will always be able to verify their identity with an employee ID number if asked
- Public Bank will never ask you to visit a non-Public Bank ATM to perform any security procedure

## Known Scam Patterns Impersonating Public Bank

- Caller claims to be from "Public Bank fraud department" and says unauthorized transactions were detected on your account
- Caller says your account is linked to money laundering and you need to transfer funds to clear your name
- Caller asks for your TAC/OTP to "cancel" or "reverse" a fraudulent transaction
- Caller claims your fixed deposit or savings account is at risk and you need to move money to a "protected account"
- Caller transfers you to a fake "Bank Negara officer" or "police officer" who demands money transfer as "bail" or "security deposit"
- Caller sends a phishing link via SMS claiming to be Public Bank, asking you to "update your account details"
- Caller claims you have an overdue loan payment and threatens legal action unless you pay immediately via transfer
- Caller pretends to be from Public Bank's credit card department, claiming your card application was misused and asking for full card details
- Caller claims your PB engage app has been hacked and asks you to share your SecureSign approval — Public Bank will never ask this
- Caller claims your eFD (fixed deposit) is maturing and you need to transfer it to a "new secure account" — Public Bank handles FD maturity automatically
- Caller says your PBe online banking will be "deactivated" unless you verify immediately — Public Bank sends written notices for account changes, not urgent phone calls

## Escalation Instructions

- If you suspect a scam call, hang up immediately
- Call Public Bank's official hotline: 1-800-22-5555

- Visit the nearest Public Bank branch with your IC
- Report to Bank Negara's BNMTELELINK: 1-300-88-5465
- Lodge a police report at the nearest police station
- Report online scams to NSRC (National Scam Response Centre): 997
