# Maybank (Malayan Banking Berhad)

## Bank Identity

- bank_id: maybank
- display_name: Maybank
- aliases: Malayan Banking, Maybank Berhad, MBB
- country: Malaysia
- official_hotline: 1-300-88-6688
- official_hotline_international: +603-7844-3696
- fraud_hotline: 03-5891-4744 (24-hour fraud reporting)
- official_website: https://www.maybank2u.com.my

## Things Maybank Will NEVER Do On a Phone Call

- Ask for your full TAC (Transaction Authorization Code) or OTP
- Ask for your Maybank2u username or password
- Ask for your full credit/debit card number, CVV, or expiry date
- Ask for your full IC number over the phone
- Ask you to transfer money to a "safe account" or "Bank Negara account"
- Ask you to install remote access apps like TeamViewer or AnyDesk
- Ask you to click a link sent via SMS to "verify your account"
- Threaten you with arrest or legal action to pressure immediate action
- Call you from a personal mobile number claiming to be Maybank staff

## Things Maybank May Legitimately Do

- Call you to verify a recent transaction flagged as suspicious
- Ask you to confirm the last 4 digits of your card number
- Ask you to verify your identity with partial IC number (last 4 digits)
- Ask basic security questions you set up during account registration
- Request you to visit a branch in person to resolve account issues
- Send official SMS from "MAE" or "Maybank" sender ID (not personal numbers)
- Ask you to call back the official hotline to continue a conversation
- Send push notifications via the MAE app for transaction alerts
- Ask you to verify a Secure2u approval request that appears on your MAE app

## Maybank-Specific Features the Caller Should Know About

If a caller claims to be from Maybank but does not know these, they may be fake:

- Maybank uses **Secure2u** (not SMS TAC) as the primary transaction approval method — a real Maybank officer would know this
- Maybank's mobile app is called **MAE by Maybank** — a fake caller might refer to it by the wrong name
- Maybank uses **Maybank2u** for online banking — the URL is maybank2u.com.my, not any other domain
- Maybank's **Kill Switch** feature lets customers instantly disable online access via the app or ATM — a real officer might suggest this, a scammer never would
- Maybank credit cards use **Mastercard/Visa 3D Secure** for online purchases, not phone-based verification

## Standard Call Procedures

- Maybank's outbound fraud alert calls will come from a recognizable Maybank number, not a personal mobile number
- Maybank will never rush you to take immediate action during a call — they will give you time to verify
- If Maybank suspects fraud on your account, they may temporarily block the card first and then contact you, not the other way around
- Maybank staff will never ask you to keep the call a secret or avoid telling family members
- Maybank will never ask you to open Maybank2u or MAE app and perform transactions while on the call with them
- Maybank fraud alerts are also sent via push notification through the MAE app — if you received a call but no app notification, be suspicious
- Maybank staff will identify themselves by name and employee ID if asked

## Known Scam Patterns Impersonating Maybank

- Caller claims to be from "Maybank security department" and says your account has been compromised
- Caller says there is a warrant for your arrest linked to your Maybank account and you must transfer funds to clear it
- Caller asks for TAC/OTP claiming they need to "verify" or "cancel" a suspicious transaction
- Caller claims your credit card was used overseas and asks for full card details to "block" it
- Caller transfers you to a fake "Bank Negara officer" or "PDRM officer" who demands money transfer
- Caller sends a link via SMS claiming to be Maybank and asks you to enter your login credentials
- Caller says you won a Maybank reward/prize and need to pay a processing fee
- Caller pretends to be from Maybank's loan department offering a pre-approved loan at suspiciously low rates, requiring upfront payment
- Caller claims your Secure2u is compromised and asks you to share the approval code — Maybank will never ask this
- Caller asks you to "upgrade" your MAE app by downloading an APK from a link — MAE is only available from official app stores
- Caller claims to be from Maybank's "Tabung Haji division" — Maybank and Tabung Haji are separate institutions

## Escalation Instructions

- If you suspect a scam call, hang up immediately
- Call Maybank's official hotline: 1-300-88-6688
- For fraud reporting: 03-5891-4744 (available 24 hours)
- Visit the nearest Maybank branch with your IC
- Report to Bank Negara's BNMTELELINK: 1-300-88-5465
- Lodge a police report at the nearest police station
- Report online scams to NSRC (National Scam Response Centre): 997
