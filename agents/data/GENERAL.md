# General Scam Detection Guidelines

This file is used as the fallback when no specific bank is identified, or when the caller does not claim to represent a bank.

## When This Applies

- The caller does not mention any specific bank
- The caller claims to be from an unrecognized or unsupported bank
- The caller claims to be from a government agency (PDRM, LHDN, Bank Negara, immigration, customs, court)
- The caller claims to be from a telco, courier company, or other non-bank organization
- The caller identity is unclear

## Universal Red Flags

Any caller — regardless of who they claim to be — is likely a scammer if they:

- Ask for your OTP, TAC, PIN, or any one-time verification code
- Ask for your full IC number, passport number, or full bank account details
- Ask you to transfer money to any account for "safekeeping," "verification," "bail," or "tax clearance"
- Ask you to install remote access apps (TeamViewer, AnyDesk, or similar)
- Threaten you with arrest, court action, deportation, or account closure
- Claim there is a warrant for your arrest linked to your bank account or phone number
- Pressure you to act immediately and not hang up
- Tell you not to inform family, friends, or your bank
- Transfer your call to someone claiming to be a police officer, Bank Negara officer, or government official
- Ask you to open your banking app or perform transactions during the call
- Send you a link via SMS or WhatsApp and ask you to click it
- Call from a personal mobile number or WhatsApp while claiming to represent an organization

## Common Non-Bank Scam Scenarios

### Fake Government Agency Calls

- Caller claims to be from PDRM (police), LHDN (tax authority), immigration, customs, or court
- Claims you are involved in money laundering, tax evasion, or criminal activity
- Demands you transfer money to "clear your name" or "post bail"
- Reality: Malaysian government agencies do not demand money over the phone and do not call to threaten arrest

### Fake Courier / Parcel Scams

- Caller claims a parcel in your name was intercepted containing drugs or contraband
- Transfers you to a fake "police officer" who demands payment
- Reality: courier companies do not call about contraband — law enforcement would visit in person

### Fake Telco Calls

- Caller claims your phone number is linked to fraud and will be terminated
- Asks for IC number or banking details to "verify" your identity
- Reality: telcos do not threaten immediate line termination over the phone

### Investment / Money Game Scams

- Caller offers guaranteed high returns on an investment
- Pressures you to invest immediately before the "opportunity" closes
- Asks for upfront payment or banking credentials
- Reality: no legitimate investment guarantees returns — this violates securities law

### Love / Social Engineering Scams

- Caller has built a relationship over time (social media, dating app)
- Asks for money for an emergency, medical bills, or travel
- Asks you to receive or transfer money on their behalf
- Reality: if someone you have never met in person asks for money, it is almost certainly a scam

### Prize / Lottery Scams

- Caller says you won a prize, contest, or lottery
- Asks for a processing fee, tax payment, or banking details to "release" the prize
- Reality: you cannot win a contest you did not enter, and legitimate prizes never require upfront payment

## Universal Safe Responses

If you are unsure whether a call is legitimate:

- Hang up immediately — a real organization will not mind
- Do not share any personal information, codes, or passwords
- Do not transfer money or make any transactions
- Do not install any apps the caller asks you to
- Do not click on any links sent during or after the call
- Call the official number of the organization the caller claimed to represent (look it up yourself, do not use a number they provide)
- Inform a trusted family member or friend about the call
- Report the call to the authorities
